Project Overview

This project, undertaken as part of my UX/UI Design journey, focuses on the redesign of Turners Car Insurance’s digital platform. Turners, a trusted name in motor vehicle sales and insurance, serves a diverse customer base seeking streamlined and transparent services. With the increasing demand for seamless and intuitive online experiences in the insurance industry, this project aims to modernize Turners' digital presence and address key user pain points. By reimagining the insurance purchase journey, integrating car purchase and insurance, and enhancing website usability, this redesign seeks to create a user-centered platform that not only improves customer satisfaction but also strengthens Turners’ position as a leader in the market.

Solution

  • Seamless Digital Journey and User-Centered Design: Simplify the car insurance purchase process by enabling users to complete the entire journey online, eliminating the need for phone calls or in-person visits. Redesign the website layout to improve navigation, making policy information, pricing, and claims processes easily accessible and visually engaging, thereby enhancing user interaction and satisfaction.

  • Transparent Comparison and Claims Tools: Introduce a robust comparison tool that allows users to evaluate insurance options based on coverage and pricing. Simplify the claims process with clear, step-by-step guidance and readily accessible policy details, fostering trust and confidence in the service.

  • Responsive and Inclusive Platform: Develop a mobile-first, fully responsive design to ensure accessibility across all devices. Incorporate modern design principles and accessibility standards to cater to diverse user needs, creating a platform that is inclusive and user-friendly.

User-Centric Redesign

The project places the needs of Turners' diverse customer base at the forefront. By understanding user frustrations, such as repetitive data entry and difficulty comparing policies, the redesign prioritizes creating a more intuitive, streamlined, and accessible platform tailored to their preferences and behaviors.

Enhanced User Experience

The insights from the UX research highlighted critical pain points in the current insurance journey. The design changes aim to improve the overall user experience by simplifying navigation, integrating insurance with car purchases, and making policy details more transparent. These changes ensure that users can seamlessly access the information they need without confusion or frustration.

Claims Process Transparency

The research revealed a significant need for a more transparent claims process. The redesign includes a step-by-step claims guide and a real-time tracking system to provide users with clarity, reducing uncertainty and building trust in the brand.

Feedback Integration

To continuously improve and strengthen customer relationships, the platform will introduce a post-purchase feedback system. This feature will allow users to provide insights into their experience, helping Turners address concerns, enhance satisfaction, and evolve based on real customer input.

Testing

Turners Cars Buying Insurance Usability Study

The primary objective of this study was to evaluate the usability of the redesigned Turners Cars Insurance process and website, focusing on enabling a seamless user experience for purchasing and managing car insurance. The study aimed to assess the design solutions against Jakob Nielsen’s 10 usability heuristics, ensuring the process met user needs related to navigation, policy comparison, and information accessibility.

Methodologies for testing

Heuristic Evaluation: Identified potential usability issues through a heuristic evaluation workshop, leveraging Jakob Nielsen's 10 usability heuristics as a framework.

Moderated Usability Testing: Conducted task-based testing to evaluate the effectiveness of design solutions. Participants were asked to perform key tasks such as purchasing insurance, comparing policies, and navigating the website.

Participants: A mix of tech-savvy users, cost-conscious individuals, and customers familiar with online insurance processes.

Metrics Tracked: Task completion rate, error rate, task completion time, and satisfaction score (Likert scale).

Key Findings in testing

Overall Usability Satisfaction: Users rated the overall usability positively, with scores between 4 and 5 on a 5-point Likert scale. Participants appreciated the streamlined process and the clarity of the policy comparison tool.

Common Suggestions:

  • Improve clarity of policy coverage descriptions by simplifying language and providing more examples.
  • Simplify navigation by reducing redundant menu options and enhancing the visibility of primary actions.
  • Add progress indicators during multi-step processes such as purchasing or updating a policy.

Conclusions for testing

The redesigned Turners Cars Insurance process and website performed well against Jakob Nielsen’s 10 usability heuristics. Strengths included effective system status visibility, error prevention, and user control. However, some opportunities for improvement were identified in areas such as clarity of information and user navigation

Recommendations for Next Steps to develop High Fidelity Wireframe

  • Refine Policy Information Layout: Use visual aids like icons and tooltips to enhance understanding of coverage options.
  • Enhance Comparisons:Improve the comparison tool with side-by-side visuals and customizable filters.
  • Simplify Navigation:Consolidate redundant menu items and prioritize key user actions like "Get a Quote" and "File a Claim."
